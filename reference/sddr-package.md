# sddr: Spatial Distribution Dynamics

A tidy toolkit for distribution dynamics: analysing how a
cross-sectional distribution of values evolves over time and where it
settles in the long run. Provides discrete-time and spatial Markov
transition estimation and ergodic (steady-state) analysis, and is being
extended with rank-mobility measures, continuous stochastic kernels,
continuous-time transitions and modern inference. Methods build on the
distribution-dynamics literature (Quah (1993)
[doi:10.1016/0014-2921(93)90031-3](https://doi.org/10.1016/0014-2921%2893%2990031-3)
; Rey (2001)
[doi:10.1111/j.1538-4632.2001.tb00444.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1538-4632.2001.tb00444.x)
), use long-format 'id'/'time'/'value' data rather than transition
matrices, and are checked for numerical parity against the reference
'giddy' library.
